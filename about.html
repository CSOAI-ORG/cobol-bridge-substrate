Where It All Began

James Castle spent decades leading Terranova Aerospace & Defence Group, overseeing mission-critical systems where failure was never an option. In that world, legacy code isn't a burden — it's the backbone of national security.

When he founded the Certified Safe AI Association (CSGA), the mission was clear: bring that same rigour to AI governance. The CASA certification framework was built on the principle that organisations deploying AI deserve the same safety assurance standards as those flying fighter jets.

Meanwhile, Nicholas Templeman was pioneering cognitive symbiosis research at the CSGA AI Research Institute — studying how humans and AI systems can work together more effectively. When Anthropic released the Model Context Protocol, both saw the same opportunity: a way to modernise COBOL safely, with built-in governance from day one.

COBOL Bridge was the result.

Why COBOL? Why Now?

Every day, 220 billion lines of COBOL process $3 trillion in global transactions. Banks, governments, healthcare systems, and defence agencies all depend on code written decades ago.

The developers who built these systems are retiring. AI can help — but only if it's governed properly. Ungoverned AI touching mission-critical COBOL is a risk no enterprise can afford.

We built COBOL Bridge to solve this exact problem: MCP-native tools with CASA certification baked in. Not governance bolted on after the fact, but AI safety assurance from the ground up.

// Timeline
The Road to COBOL Bridge

Key milestones in the journey from defence governance to AI-powered COBOL modernisation.

2018
Terranova Group Expands
James Castle's Terranova Aerospace & Defence Group reaches new markets across Europe and North America, deepening expertise in mission-critical legacy systems.
2022
CSGA Global Founded
The Certified Safe AI Association is established to create industry standards for AI safety and governance. The CASA certification framework is conceived.
2024
CASA Framework Launched
CASA (Certified AI Safety Assurance) levels CA-10 through CA-40 are published. First organisations begin certification. Nicholas Templeman joins as Chief AI Science Officer.
2025
MCP Goes Open Standard
Anthropic's Model Context Protocol is adopted by the Linux Foundation / AAIF. MCP SDK downloads hit 97 million/month. CSGA begins building MCP-native governance tools.
2025
70+ MCP Servers Published
CSGA AI Research Institute ships over 70 MCP servers across 25 enterprise sectors, establishing the largest MCP tool catalogue in the governance space.
2026
COBOL Bridge Launches
The world's first MCP-native AI governance platform for COBOL modernisation goes live. Five certified governance tools. Five enterprise sectors. One mission: bridge the gap between legacy and intelligence.
